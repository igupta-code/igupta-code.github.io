Alex Morgan

Alex Morgan became the youngest member of the U.S. woman's national soccer team in 2009. She won her first Olymic god medal by helping the U.S defeat Japan at the 2012 Summer Olypics. Alex Morgan was born on July 2, 1989 in California, but didn't start playing soccer until she was 14 years old. Morgan attended the University of California at Berkeley, at the end of her college career, she was tied for thrird on the school's list for highest scores, with 45 goals. In March of 2016, Morgan and some other teammates filed a complaint against wage discrimation against U.S. Soccer, and the topic escalated when they filed a gender discrimination. Morgan married to another pro soccer player, Servando Carrasco on December 31, 2014, and is pregnant with the couple's first child.

Megan Rapinoe

Megan Anna Rapinoe playes as a midfielder/winger for the Reign FC in the National Women's Soccer League and she is the captain of the club. She has helped the U.S. soccer team win gold at the 2012 London Olymipcs and 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup. She was the first player to score a Goal Olimpico at the Olymic Games. She has also keenled during the national anthem when alone along with Colin Kaepernick. She is also the first openly gay woman in the annal Sports Illustrated swimsuit issue. Born in Redding, California on the 5th of July, she attended Foothill High School and competed in track, backetball, and soccer. After high school, she and her fraternal twin sister attended the University of Portland on a full scholarship. She ranks 10th in the school's history with 88-point, 30 goals, 28 assists.

Kelley O'Hara

Kelley Maureen O'Hara was born on August 4,1988, and she is a FIFA Women's World Cup champion and Olympic soccer gold medalist. She playe as a wingback and midfielder for the U.S. women's national soccer team. During her time at Stanford, she played as a forward and right defender, and majored in science, technology, and society with a focus in environmental engineering. Her best season at Stanford was her senior year, where she scored 26 goals with 13 assists.
